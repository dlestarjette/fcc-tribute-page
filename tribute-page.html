Sally Ride, First American Woman & LGBT person in Space

Timeline of Dr. Ride’s life and achievements

  • 1951: Born in Los Angeles, California on May 26.
  • 1960: Sally spends a year traveling in Europe with her family. She plays tennis for the first time in Spain.
  • 1963: Ranked number 20 in Southern California for girls aged 12 and under in tennis.
  • 1968: Graduates from Westlake School for Girls in Los Angeles.
  • 1968–1970: Attends Swarthmore College.
  • 1970: Transfers to UCLA, then to Stanford as a junior.
  • 1973: Graduates from Stanford with a bachelor’s degree in Physics.
  • 1975: Earns a master’s degree in Physics from Stanford.
  • 1978: Completes a Ph.D in Physics from Stanford.
  • 1978: Selected as part of NASA Astronaut Group 8, the first class to include women.
  • 1979: Completes astronaut training and becomes eligible for spaceflight.
  • 1982: Marries Steven Hawley.
  • 1983: Becomes the first American woman in space aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger (STS-7).
  • 1984: Flies her second mission on Challenger (STS-41-G).
  • 1987: Leaves NASA.
  • 1989: Becomes professor of physics at University of California, San Diego (UCSD) and director of California Space Institute.
  • 1999–2000: President of Space.com
  • 2003: Serves on the Columbia Accident Investigation Board.
  • 2007: Retires from UCSD.
  • 2009: Serves on the Review of United States Human Space Flight Plans Committee.
  • 2011: Diagnosed with pancreatic cancer.
  • 2011: Registers domestic partnership with Tam O’Shaughnessy.
  • 2012: Sally Ride dies at home in La Jolla, California on July 23, at the age of 61.
